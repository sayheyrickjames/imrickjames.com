# Choose-Your-Own-Adventure Story: Research Questions

I'll draft the story, but I need to understand your voice first. These questions help me write something that feels authentically you—smart, funny, personal, Seth Godin-style.

---

## Your Voice & Humor

1. **What kind of funny are you?**
   - Dry/deadpan? Absurdist? Self-deprecating? Observational? Silly?
   - Example: Do you laugh at situations, wordplay, uncomfortable truths, mishaps?
   - Give me 1-2 examples of something you've written or said that made people laugh.

2. **Any sacred cows you won't joke about?**
   - Anything off-limits? (Family, politics, etc.)
   - Anything you *love* joking about?

3. **How do you like to tell stories?**
   - Linear narrative or jump around?
   - Start with the punchline or build to it?
   - Dialogue-heavy or more internal?

---

## Personal Lessons & Themes

4. **What are 3 real life lessons you've learned** that surprised you?
   - Examples: Failure, collaboration, perfectionism, risk-taking, saying no, etc.
   - For each, can you give a brief story or moment that taught you?

5. **What's something you believed at 25 that you'd tell your 25-year-old self was wrong?**
   - (This is very Seth Godin territory—discomfort, growth, hindsight)

6. **What's one embarrassing or humbling moment** you'd share publicly?
   - Not oversharing, but real enough to be relatable
   - What made it valuable or funny in retrospect?

---

## Portfolio Story Specifics

7. **If someone asks "Who are you?"** what's the real answer (not the elevator pitch)?
   - What role(s) do you play? (Designer, teacher, builder, thinker, etc.)
   - What drives you that's *not* on your résumé?

8. **What do you want visitors to *feel* when they leave your site?**
   - Inspired? Amused? Understood? Curious?
   - Should they laugh? Think? Want to hire you? Want to follow your writing?

9. **Any funny/weird client or collaboration stories?**
   - (PayPal, Medium, Stash, Plaid context) 
   - Anything absurd about working at scale? 
   - Funny design problems you've solved?

10. **What's your take on AI in design right now?** (Funny + real perspective)
    - Is it funny to you? Threatening? Liberating? All of the above?
    - Any personal stories (good or bad)?

---

## CYOA Structure Preferences

11. **What branching paths appeal to you?**
    - A) "What interests you?" → Work | Stories | Philosophy (3 paths from start)
    - B) "Pick a mindset" → Serious learner | Curious explorer | Entertainer (tone-based)
    - C) "Pick a timeline" → Past | Present | Future (chronological)
    - D) Something else?

12. **How many "endings" do you want?** (Multiple endings encourage replaying)
    - A) 3-4 endings (quick replay)
    - B) 5-7 endings (more variety)
    - C) 10+ endings (exhaustive exploration)

13. **Any topics you want woven through multiple paths?**
    - Design systems? AI? Craft? Teaching? People/collaboration?
    - Should they recur as themes?

14. **Tone across the story:**
    - Should the whole thing be funny, or is funny + serious okay?
    - Any moments where you want to be genuinely vulnerable or thoughtful?

## Seth Godin Reference

If you want a flavor of what I'm aiming for:
- Smart observations about small, real things
- Humor through truth-telling, not jokes
- Personal stories that illustrate principles (not business lessons)
- Often uncomfortable or contradictory ideas
- Generous, not cynical
- Invites reflection

**Example vibe** (not specific content):
> "Everyone tells you to be authentic. But authenticity without judgment is just chaos. The real skill is knowing which parts of yourself to share with which people."
